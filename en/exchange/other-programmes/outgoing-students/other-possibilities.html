Other possibilities

If you are considering a period of study abroad and have not chosen any of the foreign universities or other educational institutions involved in the established Charles University mobility programmes, you can try a number of other schemes: this page contains a regularly updated list of international offers. The stays listed below are organized by foreign universities or research institutions, educational foundations, institutions with links to foreign embassies, non-profit NGOs etc. Some of the stays are partly or wholly funded by the host institution; in other cases, however, the applicants are expected to cover the cost themselves.

Japan - National Graduate Institute for Policy Studies (GRIPS)

National Graduate Institute for Policy Studies (GRIPS), located in the heart of Tokyo, is an international premier policy school with the aim of contributing to the betterment of democratic governance around the world. GRIPS accepts applications to doctoral study programs starting in October 2024. The best applicants will be awarded a scholarship that covers the whole study.

Applications are submitted via online registration form.

Deadline for the program Public Finance: 15. February 2024.

Linköping University Summer Academy

Linköping University (LiU) hereby invites students to take part in the Linköping University Summer Academy!

The LiU Summer Academy will be held 28 June – 27 July 2024 in Linköping, Sweden.

Students get to choose from eight high-quality bachelor level courses (7.5 ECTS) taught in English and gain cultural insights together with other students from LiU’s international partner universities all around the globe. The LiU Summer Academy students are not affected by the Swedish Migration Agency’s extended passport control as the students will be studying in Sweden less than 90 days.

LiU strives for a good mix of students and partner universities are welcome to nominate a maximum of 5 students per course. For students from LiU’s partner universities, the LiU Summer Academy is tuition free. Along with lectures and course work, our LiU Summer Academy student hosts have organised a fun and social programme students can take part in.

Housing will be provided; a completely furnished single room with access to a fully equipped shared kitchen and a final exit cleaning together with a regional buss pass at a total cost of SEK 6 500.

Please download the questions and answers booklet for common questions about the LiU Summer Academy as well as the nomination certificate and promotional flyer.  We kindly ask you to observe our new website’s URL: https://liu.se/en/education/liu-summer-academy

2024 NATO Field School and Simulation Program

Our partner university Simon Fraser University is offering our students participation in the 2024 NATO Field School and Simulation Program. This program is run by the partner university’s Department of Political Science in collaboration with the NATO Defense College in Rome and Canada’s Joint Delegation to NATO.  It offers a unique opportunity for graduate and undergraduate students from NATO member states to immerse themselves in the fields of international relations, security, and diplomacy while embarking on an unforgettable experience across Europe, including unique and exciting destinations in Latvia, Belgium, Italy, and more!

During the 7-week summer program, students will:

  • Interact with professionals across Europe, from junior officials, soldiers, and interns, all the way to senior officials, generals, and ambassadors
  • Learn through hands-on engagement, experience, observation, practice, and professional interactions with security, defence, and diplomacy practitioners
  • Gain experience in political strategy, foreign policy formation, politico-military coordination, multilateral negotiation, strategic communication, cyber and information security, and media relations
  • Receive specialized negotiation, diplomatic, and simulation training from senior NATO officials

The deadline to apply is December 17th, 2023. To learn more, you can visit the website of the university.

If interested, you can also attend two virtual info sessions to learn more about the program, taking place on November 23rd at 9AM PST (12PM EST/6PM CET) and November 24th at 5PM PST (8PM EST). To sign up for these virtual info sessions, please see here.

The Europaeum Scholars Programme for PhD students

We draw your attention to the offer of the prestigious Europaeum university network, which offers a top educational program called "The Europaeum Scholars Programme", which is intended for PhD students, preferably in the first or second year in the field of humanities or social sciences.

Selected applicants participate in eight modules that run over two years, in parallel with their existing doctoral studies. As the program takes place in different places and institutions, it uses the resources of all 19 universities that are members of the Europaeum. Lectures and training are provided by academic staff affiliated with the Europaeum network, together with a range of external experts and leading thinkers. Program participants visit key European and international institutions and learn about their functioning through interviews with practitioners and policy makers. They then design, develop and implement their own policy-related project. At the end of the program, they can be proud of the knowledge they have gained and a set of skills and contacts that can open doors for them further in their careers.

You can find more about the program HERE and in the brochure and description of the program in the appendix.

An online information meeting for interested parties takes place on November 16, 2023. Interested parties must register HERE.

Applicants must meet the requirements listed at this link.

At the same time, he must add:

  1. You are in charge of a grant-giving organization with an annual budget of c. 1 million Euros and can decide which area or areas the organization can focus its efforts on for the next 5 years. Which areas do you choose to prioritize and why?
  2. If you had the opportunity to give professional advice to your 20-year-old self, what would it be and why?

Fullbright programmes deadlines

Current dates for Fulbright Commission programs:

The deadline for the 2nd wave of applications to the Fulbright Specialist program is December 15, 2023

Fulbright Specialist allows a 2-6 week stay of an American expert. As part of the program, the visiting Specialist can give lectures, participate in workshops, specialized academic programs and conferences or organize and lead them, help with the development and improvement of curricula and teaching materials or contribute to the training of university teachers, help with the adoption of technology, develop soft skills such as for example, academic writing, etc.

The program is not intended for individual research.

This offer applies to the traditional allocation of 5 Specialists in the Czech Republic, with partial co-financing by the host institution.

All information, including the online application and instructions, can be found at https://www.fulbright.cz/.../hostovani-americkych-akademiku/, any ambiguities and questions can be consulted, please write to fulbright@fulbright.cz

 

The deadline for the 2024/2025 research studentship is 1 February 2024

Visiting Student Researcher is an excellent opportunity for students of master's and above all doctoral programs for research projects in all fields (except clinical medicine) related to diploma or dissertation theses, to supplement research, etc.

Stays are in the range of 4-9 months at the university of your choice.

All information, including the online application and instructions, can be found at https://www.fulbright.cz/stipendia/stipendium-pro-postgradualni-studium/

Please contact Andrea Semacová semancova@fulbright.cz with questions

 

The deadline for the Fulbright-Schuman program for 2024/2025 is December 1, 2023

The Fulbright-Schuman program is intended for research projects focused on EU-US relations, issues of the EU or its institutions. It also offers a scholarship to academic administrators. The program is administered by the Fulbright Commission in Belgium, more information and contacts can be found at: https://www.fulbrightschuman.eu/grants-eu-citizens/

Winter school of University of Vienna

University of Vienna orginises its Winter school for Cultural Historical Studies 2024.

The topic of the winter school is “The Discovery of Modernity – Vienna Around 1900”. The univie: winter school takes place in Vienna, where traces of the outstanding culture of the late 19th and the beginning of the 20th century are still visible.

The two-week program combines first class academic courses with an extensive social and cultural program. Vienna’s rich cultural heritage, especially the museums, will lead to a thorough understanding of the input of the fin-de-siècle on the modernization of Europe.

This unique program consists of high-level lectures in the morning and guided excursions in the city of Vienna as well as visits to various museums in the afternoon. Outside the framework of classes, Vienna offers plenty of opportunities to explore additional cultural and historical sights.

The academic course environment encourages intercultural and social exchange and favors mutual understanding within the international student population. Participants thus broaden their horizons, meet colleagues from different fields of study, make friends for life and build connections for their future professional careers.

Knowing the academic program of your university, we believe that the courses offered at the univie: winter school for Cultural Historical Studies would complement your departments’ course offerings perfectly.

Given the intercultural and interdisciplinary aspect of the program our course offerings are, without question, of interest for students from all fields of study, but certainly of special interest for students who study one of the following fields: History, Cultural Studies, Art, Design, Architecture, Literature, Psychology, and Political Science.

The univie: winter school 2024 will take place from February 2 – 16, 2024.

Winter School fees are € 1,460 and include registration, tuition, and all planned excursions. (External students have to pay the Austrian students' union fee of € 22.70 to be admitted to the University of Vienna and to enjoy insurance coverage).

The application deadline for the winter school is December 15, 2023.

Please have a look at our short video for an overview of the program: Winter School Video (univie.ac.at)

Attached you will find the PDF version of the folder for 2024.

For more detailed information please visit our website: https://shs.univie.ac.at/winter-school/

If you have any questions or special requests, please contact the Program Coordinators at: sommerhochschule@univie.ac.at
